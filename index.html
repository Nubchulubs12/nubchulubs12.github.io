How this mortgage calculator works

This mortgage calculator estimates your monthly payment based on the loan amount, interest rate (APR), loan term, and optional down payment. It uses a standard loan amortization formula commonly used by mortgage lenders to estimate a fixed monthly payment.

Enter your numbers above and click Calculate Payment to see your estimated monthly payment, total interest paid over the loan term, and the total amount paid.

Understanding your results

  • Loan Principal: The amount you borrow after subtracting your down payment from the loan amount.
  • Total Interest: The total interest paid over the full loan term (principal excluded).
  • Total Payment: The total amount paid over the loan term (principal + interest).

Mortgage calculator FAQ

Is this calculator accurate?
This calculator provides estimates. Your lenderâ€™s exact payment can differ based on fees, escrow, taxes, insurance, and your final interest rate.

Does this include taxes and insurance?
No. Taxes and insurance vary widely. Many lenders include these in your monthly payment through escrow.

Can I use this for refinancing?
Yes. Enter your remaining balance as the loan amount, your new rate, and the new term to estimate a refinance payment.

Why does interest cost so much over time?
Interest adds up over long loan terms, especially at higher rates. Shorter terms usually cost less in total interest, but have higher monthly payments.

How mortgage payments are calculated

Mortgage payments are calculated using an amortization schedule, which spreads loan repayment over a fixed number of monthly payments. Early payments consist mostly of interest, while later payments pay down more principal.

Factors that affect your monthly payment include the loan amount, interest rate, loan term length, and whether you make a down payment. Even small changes to the interest rate can significantly impact the total interest paid over time.
